üìñ About Ilocano Language

Ilocano (Ilokano) is the third most-spoken native language in the Philippines, primarily in the Ilocos Region and northern Luzon. Pronunciation is generally phonetic with vowels similar to Spanish: A (ah), E (eh), I (ee), O (oh), U (oo). The language uses some Spanish loanwords from colonial history.

üéØ Pronunciation Tips for Americans

Vowels: A (ah), E (eh), I (ee), O (oh), U (oo) - pronounced clearly like Spanish
"ng" sound: Very common in Ilocano, like "ng" in "sing"
Double vowels: Hold the sound longer (e.g., "aa" = longer "ah")
Stress: Usually on the second-to-last syllable
R: Lightly rolled or tapped, not as strong as Spanish R
Spanish influence: Many Spanish loanwords from colonial period

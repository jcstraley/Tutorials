üìñ Pronunciation Guide

Tips for Americans: Tagalog pronunciation is generally straightforward for English speakers. Vowels are pronounced as in Spanish: A (ah), E (eh), I (ee), O (oh), U (oo). The "ng" sound (as in "sing") is common and can start words. R's are lightly rolled or flapped.

üéØ Quick Pronunciation Tips

NG: Like "ng" in "sing" - can start words (e.g., ngayon)
R: Lighter than English R, almost like a light tap
Stress: Usually on the last or second-to-last syllable
Vowels: A=ah, E=eh, I=ee, O=oh, U=oo (like Spanish)
': Apostrophe indicates dropped letters (e.g., sa'yo = sa iyo)

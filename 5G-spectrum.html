Executive Summary

Organizations can obtain exclusive access to licensed 5G spectrum in the United States without FCC auctions by acquiring spectrum rights from existing license holders via secondary market transactions. This guide outlines key bands, legal routes, step-by-step procedures, and major companies in the spectrum marketplace.

Spectrum Acquisition Methods

1. Full Assignment (License Transfer)

  1. Identify target spectrum/license holders in the FCC ULS database.
  2. Negotiate purchase agreement and conduct due diligence (CBRS PAL: $5,000–$50,000+/county).
  3. Prepare and file FCC Form 603 and, if required, FCC Form 602.
  4. FCC review and public notice (30 days), approval in 21–90 days.
  5. Consummate transaction and update ULS; new owner assumes obligations.

2. Spectrum Manager Lease

  1. Identify and contact license holders directly; define lease scope/terms.
  2. Negotiate pricing ($10,000–$500,000+/year depending on band and area).
  3. File FCC Form 608 electronically.
  4. FCC acceptance and informational public notice (typically 0–5 days).
  5. Commence network operations post-acceptance.

3. CBRS Priority Access License (PAL) Acquisition

  1. Search FCC ULS for PAL licenses (Service Code: CW), identify holders.
  2. Contact PAL holders for assignment or lease (Lease: $1,000–$10,000+/PAL/year).
  3. Complete FCC filing process as per assignment or lease method above.
  4. Register with a CBRS Spectrum Access System (SAS) provider such as Federated Wireless, CommScope, or Google.
  5. Deploy CBRS-compliant network within licensed boundaries.

Regional Spectrum Holders & WISPs

  1. Use FCC ULS database to identify local licensees (search by band and county).
  2. Contact via company website, email, or phone (often CTO/CEO for smaller entities).
  3. If direct contact fails, engage a broker (usually 3–10% transaction fee).
